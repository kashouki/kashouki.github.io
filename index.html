About

Hello, I'm Daniel. I'm based in Taipei and currently work as a software engineer at Sunbird Software. My day-to-day focus is backend development, primarily using Java. Beyond building new features, I collaborate closely with frontend developers and QA engineers to ensure everything runs smoothly. I also take on customer-reported issues, tracking down bugs and resolving them before they cause too much mischief.

I hold a master's degree in Electrical Engineering from National Taiwan University, where my research centered on haptic mechanisms in virtual reality. This meant plenty of time spent in Unity, along with prototyping devices using Arduino — essentially, finding ways to make the digital world a little more “touchable.” On the side, I tutored high school students in Java, which is a fun way to pass on what I've learned.

When I'm off the clock, you'll likely find me wandering the city streets. If the weather insists otherwise, I retreat indoors to Splatsville for a good round of squid splatting, or clocking in some “volunteer overtime” at Grizzco Industries.

Languages are another passion of mine. I'm fluent in Mandarin Chinese and English, and currently studying Spanish, Japanese, and French, with plans to pursue official proficiency certifications.
